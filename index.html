Platforms



We have three scanning platforms:

1. Inspector Gadget (IG) : this is our continuous scanning platform. It continously performs localization and mapping as it drives around for fastest mapping results and best area coverage. This product is still in early stages of development.




2. Robot Eng (RobEn) : this is our stop-and-go scanning solution. This robot must stop to take scans (<10s/scan). This scanner is very rapid compared to the Faro but not as quick as IG. The mapping and full colourization of the map is done automatically within minutes of taking the scan. This product is in its final stages of development.




3. Faro Focus : We also own a more traditional tripod based laser scanner (often referred to as Terrestrial Laser Scanners). This is the current go-to solution for most land-based laser scanning. These scans are very time consuming and manual data-processing is required after collecting the scans to generate the final maps.

Mapping with the Autonomoose



We also have some maps generated using data from the University of Waterloo's autonomous vehicle platform, the Autonomoose. This work was done in collaboration with the Waterloo Autonomous Vehicles (WAVE) lab. Specifically, we lead research on filtering snowfall from Lidar scans for autonomous vehicles. We developped a custom de-noising filter to remove snow in real-time without removing important environmental features. This work was published and presented at the Computer and Robot Vision (CRV) conference in 2018 (see paper here).
